Chanamasla (Savory Indian Chick Peas)

INGREDIENTS REQUIRE :

1 onion, chopped 1 tomato, chopped 1 (1 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and chopped 4 cloves garlic, chopped, or more to taste 1 green chile pepper, seeded and chopped (Optional) 3 tablespoons olive oil 2 fresh bay leaves 1 teaspoon chili powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1 teaspoon garam masala ½ teaspoon turmeric powder 1 pinch salt to taste water as needed 1 (15 ounce) can chickpeas 1 teaspoon fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish, or more to taste

About:

Chana masala (also chole masala, or chole) is a chickpea curry originating in the Indian subcontinent. It is a staple dish in North Indian cuisine.It is often eaten with a deep fried bread called bhatura.

PROCESS:

Heat the Oil: In a large pan, heat the oil over medium heat.
Sauté Cumin Seeds: Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
Add Onions: Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add Garlic and Ginger: Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
Add Green Chilies: If using, add the green chilies and sauté for another minute.
Add Tomatoes: Add the tomato puree or chopped tomatoes. Cook until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate.
Add Spices: Mix in the chana masala powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Stir well to combine.
Add Chickpeas: Add the cooked chickpeas and stir to coat them with the spice mixture.
Add Water: Pour in water to adjust the consistency. Bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat and let it cook for 10-15 minutes. Stir occasionally.
Adjust Seasoning: Taste and adjust salt or spices as needed.
Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti, and lemon wedges on the side.
