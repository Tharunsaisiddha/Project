INGREDIENTS REQUIRE :

For the Gravy: 200g paneer (cut into cubes) 1 cup fresh or frozen green peas 2 tbsp oil or ghee 1 large onion (chopped) 2 medium tomatoes (pureed) 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste 1-2 green chilies (slit, optional for extra spice) ½ cup water (adjust as needed) Spices: 1 tsp cumin seeds 1 tsp turmeric powder 1½ tsp coriander powder 1 tsp garam masala ½ tsp red chili powder Salt to taste ¼ cup cream or cashew paste (optional, for a richer gravy) Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

About:

Matar Paneer is a beloved dish in Indian cuisine, particularly in North India. It features two primary ingredients: matar (green peas) and paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cooked in a rich and spiced tomato-based gravy. Here’s more about this dish, its significance, and its variations.

PROCESS:

Heat the Oil: Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle
Sauté Onions: Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
Add Garlic and Ginger: Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
Add Tomatoes: Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and start breaking down.
Spices: Mix in the curry powder, turmeric, cumin seeds, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are fragrant.
Add Paneer: Add the paneer cubes to the gravy. Stir gently to coat the paneer with the gravy.
Add Panner and Simmer: Add garam masala and cream or cashew paste if using, and simmer for another 2-3 minutes until the gravy reaches your desired consistency.
Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with naan.
