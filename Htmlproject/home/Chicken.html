Indian Chicken Curry

INGREDIENTS REQUIRE :

2 tbsp sunflower oil. 1 onion, thinly sliced. 2 garlic cloves, crushed. thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated. 6 chicken thighs, boneless and skinless. 3 tbsp medium spice paste (tikka works well) 400g can chopped tomatoes. 100g Greek yogurt.

About:

Chicken curry or curry/curried chicken is a South Asian dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is common in the cuisine of the Indian subcontinent, Caribbean, Southeast Asia, Great Britain, and Kenya. A typical curry from the Indian subcontinent consists of chicken stewed in an onion- and tomato-based sauce, flavoured with ginger, garlic, tomato puree, chilli peppers and a variety of spices, often including turmeric, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cardamom. Outside of South Asia, chicken curry is often made with a pre-made spice mixture known as curry powder.

PROCESS:

Heat the Oil: In a large pot or pan, heat the oil over medium heat.
Sauté Onions: Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
Add Garlic and Ginger: Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
Add Tomatoes: Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and start breaking down.
Spices: Mix in the curry powder, turmeric, cumin seeds, and salt. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are fragrant.
Add Chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the pot. Stir well to coat them in the spice mixture. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the chicken is browned on all sides.
Add Coconut Milk: Pour in the coconut milk (or water) and bring to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let it cook for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
Adjust Seasoning: Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan.
