INGREDIENTS REQUIRE :

For Idli: 1 cup idli rice ¼ cup urad dal (split black gram) 1 tbsp poha (flattened rice) or cooked rice (for softness) Salt to taste Water as needed For Sambar: ½ cup toor dal (pigeon pea lentils) 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrot, potato, brinjal, drumstick, etc.) 1 onion (sliced) 1 tomato (chopped) 2 green chilies (slit) 2 tbsp sambar powder Tamarind pulp (a small lemon-sized ball soaked in water) Salt to taste 1 tsp turmeric powder For Tempering: 1 tbsp oil or ghee 1 tsp mustard seeds ½ tsp cumin seeds 2 dried red chilies 1 pinch asafoetida (hing) 10-12 curry leaves Coriander leaves for garnish

About:

Idli is a soft, steamed rice cake, made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram lentils), known for being light, fluffy, and easily digestible. Sambar, on the other hand, is a lentil-based stew cooked with a variety of vegetables, tamarind, and aromatic spices. Combining the two creates Sambar Idli, a comforting, nutritious dish that’s well-loved across India, particularly in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka.

PROCESS:

Prepare Idli Batter: Rinse the rice and urad dal in water and soak for atleast 4-5 hours.
Pour Batter: Pour the Batter into greased idli molds.
Cook Dal:Rinse the toor dal, and pressure cook with turmeric powder and water until soft.Mash the dal and set aside.
Cook Vegetables: Heat oil in a pan, add sliced onions, and cook until soft. Add tomatoes and cook until they break down. Then add carrots, potatoes, and drumsticks, along with salt and sambar powder.
Combine: Add the mashed dal, tamarind paste, and water (adjust consistency as per preference). Simmer for 10-15 minutes until vegetables are tender and flavors blend.
Prepare Tempering:Heat oil or ghee in a small pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, dried red chil
Serve the Sambar Idli: Place 2-3 idlis on a plate, pour a generous amount of hot sambar over them, Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve with a side of coconut chutney if desired.
